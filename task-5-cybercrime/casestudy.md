# Case Study: Phishing Attack on Students

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal personal information such as passwords, bank details, or account data by pretending to be a trusted person or organization. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look real but are actually created by cybercriminals.

A common example of phishing occurs when students receive an email that appears to be from their school or a popular online service. The email usually says something urgent, such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Update your details immediately.” The message also includes a link that looks genuine. When the student clicks on the link, it opens a fake website that looks exactly like the original one. The student then enters their username, password, or other personal details. Once the information is entered, it is directly sent to the cybercriminal.

Students and young people are often targeted because they use the internet frequently and may not always check whether a website or email is real. The consequences of phishing can be serious. A student’s social media account can be hacked, personal photos can be misused, and in some cases, bank accounts or online payment apps can also be affected.

This case study shows the importance of digital awareness. Students should always check the email address, avoid clicking on unknown links, and never share personal information online without verifying the source.
